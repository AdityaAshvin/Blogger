Life Is About Choices; Choosing To Read This Could Change Your Life!

  • Linal
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12

Our very existence is based on a choice. That choice was typically made by our biological parents. Consequently, the lives we lead are based on what we choose and when we choose it. Who you are today, is based on how you chose yesterday. Who you become tomorrow, is based on your choices today. Based on this premise, the question you should always be asking yourself is: Am I you choosing powerfully with intention or by default? Yes, default is a choice!

Consider that when you are not choosing, you still are choosing, because not choosing is a choice.

The tapestry of our lives are woven by the consequence of our choices! What have you chosen? Have you chosen to know your purpose? Have you chosen to live your purpose? Have you chosen to pursue your dreams? Have you chosen action in the face of your fear? Have you chosen to be a doer and not just a dreamer? What are you choosing? Are you choosing comfort? Are you choosing entertainment? Are you choosing to read that book? Are you choosing discomfort on purpose in service of your goals and aspirations? What choices are you making? Have you chosen to live the life you intend to live?

How Important Is Face To Face Communication In Business?

We’re living in a digital age, where we rely on technology in our social lives, in our relationships and at work. Between email, social media apps, instant messaging, and video chat, there’s no denying how valuable technology has become for communicating with friends, family and co-workers.
In the workplace, agile working isn’t a new concept. Many businesses have embraced a more flexible approach to work; enabling their employees to work where, when and how best suits the work they are doing. Technology has been key to agile working, with software like Slack, Skype and Zoom facilitating virtual meetings, enabling staff to collaborate remotely, and allowing management to send important messages with the click of a button.

In future, will businesses revert to mostly face to face communication, or will virtual working still be a key part of our lives? And moving forward, will every business need to embrace a more agile approach?
